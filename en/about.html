About

A place to find ourselves and others through music, prose, poetry, dance and painting/drawing. A place to remember traditions, musicians and writers. A place to enjoy culture in all it’s forms and to meet people that share the same interests. Cultural evening is the place where we understand our individual and shared history, we discuss and share art and cultural experiences, as we consider them chronicles of our own lives.

When

We meet periodically to enjoy and share music & poetry recitals, prose, painting, photography, traditions, life stories and historical events of people & places. The meetings usually take place at the Naperville Public Library.

Occasionally (Mother’s day, International Women's Day, International Children’s day, Christmas, etc.) we choose different locations, in accordance with the event. To find out about our future events, visit NPL's website or e-mail us.

Who

Maria Theodorescu

Education professional teaching in Chicago Area School Districts 225 & 204, Maria Theodorescu earned her Masters degree in Management & Business Administration at the University of Economic Studies in Bucharest, and an Instructor certification within the Research & Development Accredited Program of National Education Ministry, Romania. Aside from teaching, she managed local World Cafe multicultural events as PDAC Advisory Council member & School Liaison in Naperville School District 204. At present, she is involved in several local cultural projects like Summer School classes and icon paintings courses. Starting 2017 to present, she manages "Romanian Cultural Evening" (Seara Culturala Romaneasca) - an event series organized mainly at Naperville Public Library.

Raluca Bojor

Romanian musician and academician, currently resides in the greater Chicago Area. Her education includes a Bachelor's of Music from Wheaton College (Wheaton, IL) and Master's of Arts in History of Christianity from the same institution. Raluca is pursuing a doctorate at Trinity Evangelical Divinity School and her dissertation research will focus on the Protestant Reformation in Brașov, Romania in the sixteenth century. Aside from her academic studies, Raluca teaches piano lessons, performs in local concerts and most recently launched her career as a recording artist.
