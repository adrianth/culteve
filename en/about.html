What

A place to find ourselves and others through poetry, prose, music, dance and painting/drawing. A place to remember traditions, poets and writers. A place to enjoy art and culture in all it’s forms and to meet people that share the same interests. Cultural evening is the place where, through art and culture activities, we understand our individual and shared history, we discuss and share art and cultural experiences, as we consider them chronicles of our own lives or the lives of others.

When

We meet periodically (October through May), and we enjoy poetry, prose, music recitals, painting/drawing/photography presentations, we share traditions, life stories and historical events of places/countries throughout history. The meetings ussualy take place at the Naperville Public Library.

On specific occasions (Mother’s day, International Children’s day etc., Christmas), we meet in other locations that will be announced a month before the event.

Who

Marie Theodorescu, is an Education Professional in Glenview & Naperville, IL. Besides teaching and advocating within School District's Advisory Council, Marie manages local World Cafe multicultural event series, to address equity, diversity and inclusion in local schools and among community members. She is involved in several other cultural projects organized at Naperville Public Library or/and within local churches: icon painting classes, Sunday School classes, and "Romanian Cultural Evening" event series. In her free time, Marie paints landscapes and icons. Although at present, due to COVID-19, cultural public gatherings are postponed, in the future she will continue promoting and managing multicultural events within schools and local communities, hopefully starting 2021.
