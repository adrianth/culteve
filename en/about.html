What

A place to find ourselves and others through poetry, prose, music, dance and painting/drawing. A place to remember traditions, poets and writers. A place to enjoy art and culture in all it’s forms and to meet people that share the same interests. Cultural evening is the place where, through art and culture activities, we understand our individual and shared history, we discuss and share art and cultural experiences, as we consider them chronicles of our own lives or the lives of others.

When

We meet periodically (October through May), and we enjoy poetry, prose, music recitals, painting/drawing/photography presentations, we share traditions, life stories and historical events of places/countries throughout history. The meetings ussualy take place at the Naperville Public Library.

On specific occasions (Mother’s day, International Children’s day etc., Christmas), we meet in different locations, depending on the event. If you are interested to participate with your artistic contribution or if you enjoy art and culture in general, contact us through e-mail. You will be anounced privately about future events, or visit Naperville Public Library's events public facebook page.

Who

Maria Theodorescu

Education Professional teaching and advocating in Chicago Area's School Districts 225 & 204, Maria Theodorescu has a Master degree in Management and Business Administration and a Managerial Economics Instructor certification within The Research and Development CEDES Accredited Program of National Education Ministry, Romania. Aside from teaching, she managed local World Cafe multicultural events as PDAC Advisory Council member and School Liaison in Naperville School District 204. PDAC's main goal is to address equity, diversity and inclusion in schools and among community members. In the recent past, she was involved in several local cultural projects as icon painting classes, Sunday School classes, and crafts & paintings exibitions. Starting 2018 to present, she is managing "Romanian Cultural Evening" event series, mainly organized at Naperville Public Library. More details about & contact

Raluca Bojor Hofmeyer

Romanian musician and academician, Raluca Bojor Hofmeyer currently resides in the greater Chicago Area. Her education includes a Bachelor's of Music from Wheaton College (Wheaton, IL) and Master's of Arts in History of Christianity from the same institution. She is pursuing a doctorate at Trinity Evangelical Divinity School and her dissertation research will focus on the Protestant Reformation in Brașov, Romania in the sixteenth century. Aside from her academic studies, Raluca teaches piano lessons, performs in local concerts and most recently launched her career as a recording artist. Website – www.ralucabojor.com
